    Physics of the oceanic boundary layer

    The dynamics of the ocean's boundary layer are dominated by forcing from the atmosphere. Atmospheric forcing includes momentum forcing by winds, salinity forcing by evaporation and precipitation, and heat forcing by latent heat fluxes, sensible heat fluxes, and incoming and outgoing radiation.
